Fettuccine Alfredo

Description

Fettuccine Alfredo is a classic Italian pasta dish known for its rich and creamy sauce made from butter, heavy cream, and Parmesan cheese. The sauce beautifully coats wide fettuccine noodles, creating a luscious, silky texture. The dish is often seasoned simply with salt, pepper, and sometimes garlic for extra flavor. It's quick to prepare and can be served as a comforting main course or paired with grilled chicken, shrimp, or vegetables. Traditionally garnished with fresh parsley, Fettuccine Alfredo is beloved for its simplicity and indulgence.

Ingredients

  • Fettuccine pasta (12 oz)
  • Butter (1/2 cup or 1 stick)
  • Heavy cream (1 cup)
  • Garlic (2 cloves, minced, optional)
  • Parmesan cheese (1 cup, grated)
  • Salt (to taste)
  • Ground black pepper (to taste)
  • Fresh parsley (for garnish, optional)

Steps

  1. Cook the pasta: Boil a large pot of salted water and cook the fettuccine pasta according to package instructions until al dente. Drain and set aside.
  2. Prepare the sauce: In a large skillet over medium heat, melt the butter. If using, add the minced garlic and saut√© for 1-2 minutes until fragrant.
  3. Add the cream: Pour in the heavy cream and bring to a simmer, stirring occasionally. Let it cook for about 2-3 minutes until it slightly thickens.
  4. Add the cheese: Stir in the grated Parmesan cheese and continue to stir until the cheese is fully melted and the sauce becomes smooth. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  5. Toss the pasta: Add the cooked fettuccine to the skillet and toss it with the Alfredo sauce until the pasta is well coated.
  6. Garnish and serve: Garnish with fresh parsley, if desired, and serve immediately while the pasta is hot and creamy.
