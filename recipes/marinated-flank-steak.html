Marinated Flank Steak

Description

Marinated flank steak is a flavorful and tender dish made by soaking a flank steak in a rich marinade to infuse it with flavor before grilling or searing. Flank steak, a lean cut of beef, benefits from marinating as it helps to tenderize the meat and enhance its taste. The marinade typically includes a mix of acidic ingredients like lime juice or vinegar, combined with oil, soy sauce, and seasonings like garlic, honey, and herbs. After marinating, the steak is cooked quickly at high heat and sliced thinly against the grain, making it perfect for dishes like fajitas, salads, or sandwiches.

Ingredients

  • Flank steak (1.5 to 2 lbs)
  • Olive oil (1/4 cup)
  • Soy sauce (1/4 cup)
  • Red wine vinegar (2 tablespoons)
  • Worcestershire sauce (2 tablespoons)
  • Honey (1 tablespoon)
  • Garlic (3 cloves, minced)
  • Fresh lime juice (2 tablespoons)
  • Ground black pepper (1 teaspoon)
  • Salt (to taste)
  • Red pepper flakes (optional, 1/2 teaspoon for heat)
  • Fresh herbs (optional, such as rosemary or thyme, 1 tablespoon chopped)

Steps

  1. Prepare the marinade: In a bowl or resealable bag, combine olive oil, soy sauce, red wine vinegar, Worcestershire sauce, honey, minced garlic, lime juice, black pepper, salt, and optional red pepper flakes or herbs. Whisk everything together until well blended.
  2. Marinate the steak: Place the flank steak into the marinade, ensuring it's fully coated. Seal the bag or cover the dish and refrigerate for at least 2 hours, or preferably overnight for maximum flavor.
  3. Preheat the grill or pan: When ready to cook, heat a grill to medium-high or preheat a cast-iron skillet over medium-high heat.
  4. Cook the steak: Remove the steak from the marinade, allowing excess liquid to drip off. Grill or sear the steak for 4-6 minutes per side, depending on your desired level of doneness (medium-rare is recommended).
  5. Rest the steak: Once cooked, remove the flank steak from the grill or pan and let it rest for 5-10 minutes to allow the juices to redistribute.
  6. Slice and serve: Slice the steak thinly against the grain for tender pieces. Serve with your favorite sides like roasted vegetables, potatoes, or a salad.
